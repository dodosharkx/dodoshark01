 0.2–12 tonnes/hour

 Working Principle:
 Based on the high-speed relative motion between the rotating tooth disc and the stationary tooth disc. After entering the crushing chamber through the feed inlet, the material undergoes intense impact, shearing and grinding between the rotating teeth and the stationary teeth. Simultaneously, the material particles collide and rub against one another, rapidly breaking down into fine powder. Finally, the material is discharged through the screen under the action of airflow or centrifugal force, thereby achieving particle size control.
 Summary of Features:
 The claw-type Grinder is the most widely used high-efficiency impact and grinding crushing equipment in the agricultural and food processing sectors. It has a broader range of applications than hammer-type Grinders. It accommodates a wider range of materials and offers a broader fineness range. It achieves higher efficiency in processing fine powders.
 Customers may choose either a standalone unit or a large-scale production line.

 ④ DoDoShark Ultra-Fine Grinder

 100–300 mesh (particle size 50–150 micrometres)
 (Air classification)
 Matching Motor Power: 15 kW–150 kW
 Material:
 A: Cast iron
 B: 304 stainless steel
 corn Output Range:
 50 kg–1.5 tonnes/hour
 (Air screening)
 Working Principle:
 The material is fed into the crushing chamber via the feeding device, where it is crushed through collision, shearing, friction and grinding under the impact of hammers and airflow. The crushed material is carried by an upward air current into the classification chamber for grading. Qualified fine powder passes through the classification impeller, exits via the discharge pipe and is collected by the collector, whilst unqualified fine powder is flung out by the classification impeller and returns to the crushing chamber for re-crushing.
 Key Features:
 Primarily used for applications requiring a fineness of 100–300 mesh, offering a suitable energy efficiency ratio. Widely applicable for ultra-fine grinding in industries such as chemicals, pharmaceuticals, animal feed, plastics, food and non-metallic minerals.
 Some materials can be processed to a fineness of over 1000 mesh.
