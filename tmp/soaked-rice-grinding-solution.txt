High-efficiency Rice Milling Solution Using DoDoShark
 Abstract
 [Image of Soaked Rice]
This solution is specifically tailored to meet the high-efficiency milling requirements for soaked rice (wet rice), addressing the industry challenges associated with traditional equipment, such as clogging, screen fouling and low throughput when processing wet materials.Leveraging three core technologies—stainless steel construction, a widened chamber structure and a high-volume air-cooling system—DoDoShark ensures stable processing of soaked rice. It offers selectable mesh sizes ranging from 0.4 to 0.8 mm (fineness approximately 80–120 mesh), with a maximum hourly output of over 3 tonnes per unit.The solution features a fully dust-free design covering the entire process from feeding and grinding to collection, ensuring food-grade hygiene standards. It helps rice product processing enterprises (producing rice cakes, rice noodles, rice vermicelli, tangyuan flour, etc.) to increase production capacity, reduce the frequency of downtime for cleaning, and stabilise product quality.

 Analysis of Soaked Rice Characteristics
 Characteristics
 Detailed Description
 Challenges for the Milling Process
 High moisture content
 After soaking, the moisture content of the rice grains reaches 30%–40%
 The wet material is highly viscous and prone to clogging the screen
 Tendency for starch gelatinisation
 Heat generated during the grinding process can easily cause starch gelatinisation
 Once gelatinised, the material clumps together, causing the screen to become easily clogged
 Grain expansion
 Rice grains absorb water and swell, increasing in volume
 A decrease in throughput per unit volume
 Increased toughness
 The toughness of the rice grains increases after soaking
 Increased difficulty in grinding, leading to higher energy consumption
 Strict hygiene requirements
 Used in food processing; hygiene standards must be met
 Equipment must be easy to clean and free of hard-to-reach areas

 Problems with Conventional Grinding Solutions
 Problem Dimensions
 Pain Points of Traditional Solutions
 Impact on Customers
 Screens clog easily
 Wet rice is highly adhesive, causing standard screens to clog within minutes
 Frequent shutdowns for cleaning, resulting in a drop in production capacity of over 50%
 Starch Gelatinisation

 Low production capacity

 Unable to meet the demands of large-scale production
 Hygiene risks

 Microbial growth poses food safety risks
 Motor overload
 High resistance when grinding wet materials leads to motor burnout
 High equipment failure rates and high maintenance costs
 Dust issues
 Although wet materials produce less dust, they generate significant moisture, rendering standard dust extraction ineffective
 Damp workshop conditions lead to equipment corrosion
